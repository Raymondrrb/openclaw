# Natural Language Corpus — Phrases to Emulate

Extracted from benchmark video transcripts. Use these as reference when writing scripts.
Updated: 2026-02-16. Sources: TechVisions, Dave2D, BTODtv, Performance Reviews, Elliot Page, Ahnestly.

## Hook Techniques

### Confusion/Overwhelm (TechVisions style)

- "Buying a [category] in 2026 is more confusing than ever?"
- "[Jargon 1], [jargon 2], [jargon 3], [jargon 4]. So, which one is actually worth your money?"

### Confident Authority (Dave2D style)

- "For 2026, the [brand] lineup has some very cool [category]. We're going to start off with this one right here."
- No question. No confusion. Just confident statement + immediate product.

### Specific + Honest (BTODtv style)

- "In this video, we're going to pick our best [category] for [specific use case]. That means [specific numbers]."
- Defines the scope immediately. No vague promises.
- Adds personal credibility: heights, experience, physical presence

### Anti-Shill Expert (Performance Reviews style)

- "[Channel], where I give you the review from the [expert role]'s point of view."
- "today we're going to be making a list of [products] you may never have heard of or even seen advertised."
- "if you're looking for another shill for one of the big [category] companies, this is not that video."
- Establishes niche authority + contrarian angle in hook itself.

### Meta-Complaint Hook (Ahnestly style — best for short-form)

- "hey honestly I love your videos but I don't have time to watch a 2-hour long [category] review just tell me what is the best [product]"
- The hook IS the viewer's frustration. Immediately answers the question. Zero buildup.

### NEVER use:

- "Are you looking for the best [X]?"
- "In this video, we will look at..."
- "Before we get started..."

## Product Intro Phrases (vary every product)

- "[Product] earns the crown of best [X] because it delivers..."
- "[Product] earns the title best for [use case] in our 2026 lineup because..."
- "[Product] stands out. That's why we call it our best [X] for 2026."
- "We're going to start off with this one right here."
- "These are the [adjective] [category] from [brand]."
- "Right when you power it up, you'll feel that this isn't your average [X]."
- "So, my pick here is going to be the [product]." (BTODtv — casual ownership)
- "I actually ended up picking the [product], but there's a little bit of an asterisk." (BTODtv — sets up nuance)
- "[Product] is probably one of the most underrated, undertalked about [category] in the entire market." (BTODtv — contrarian discovery)

## Transition Phrases (between products)

- "All right, let's talk about the [product]." (Dave2D — casual pivot)
- "All right, I'm going to move on to the [product]." (Dave2D — transparent)
- No transition at all — just start the next product with its award title (TechVisions)
- "All right, at $[price], what do you have here?" (BTODtv — price tier as transition)
- "All right, the final pick. No budget pick." (BTODtv — builds anticipation)
- Viewer question as transition: "okay I don't want to spend that much, do you have anything cheaper?" (Ahnestly — need-driven)

### NEVER use:

- "Next up, we have..."
- "Moving on to number [X]..."
- "Let's dive into..."

## Trust Phrases (honest limitations)

### Pivots into limitation:

- "But yes, it's not perfect."
- "Of course, no [X] is perfect."
- "There are still trade-offs."
- "It's not for everyone, but I think most people would find it useful."

### Radical honesty (BTODtv style — strongest trust builder):

- "I'm going to be real honest. [Negative truth]."
- "[Cheap option] and [use case], they don't mix."
- "That's just the truth."
- "Do not buy the [accessory]. You are going to regret it."
- "is it meant to be [use case]? No, it's not."

### Limitation delivery:

- "viewing angles are a bit limited — if you sit wide off center, picture quality degrades."
- "it's a little heavier and thicker than your average [X]"
- "we're going to have to see how that holds up in future reviews"
- "if you [specific use case], there is a [specific risk]"
- "if you're maybe [height/size], you're probably going to want to look at [alternative]" (BTODtv — body-specific)

### NEVER use:

- "The only downside is..." (too formulaic)
- "On the negative side..." (sounds like a review template)

## Opinion Markers (personal authority)

### Strong, unhedged:

- "This is sick."
- "This is awesome."
- "still one of my favorite [category] on the market"
- "this is how [X] should be done"
- "It's ridiculously powerful. Complete overkill."

### Personal experience anchors (BTODtv style — highest trust):

- "I sit in it in my office. I sit in it at home. I'm sitting in it right now."
- "I'm already using this [product] [X] hours and I love it."
- "My favorite [category] of all time is [product]."

### Positioned beliefs:

- "I've always said this — if you can get more [X] in the same footprint, it's better."
- "I focused on real performance, long-term value, and features that actually matter."

### Genuine surprise/confession (Performance Reviews style):

- "I was surprised at how much better it was than when I saw it [elsewhere] — it just kind of blew me away."
- "I even gave one to my mother-in-law, so that should say something right there."
- "Which one do I actually use every day? [Unexpected honest answer]."

### Casual slang punches (Elliot Page style):

- "Those are bangers."
- "They look sick."
- "Super cozy."
- "It's almost a no-brainer at that point."

### Product-as-object language (Ahnestly style):

- "this thing is fantastic" — not "this product offers a fantastic experience"
- "this thing has a fat seat cushion" — direct, physical
- "built like a tank" — vivid metaphor, zero jargon

### NEVER use:

- "It's worth noting that..." (hedge)
- "It boasts..." (press release)
- "This product is a game-changer" (cliche)

## Audience Direct Address

- "Gamers and action lovers, listen up." (TechVisions — targets specific segment)
- "just imagine working on if this is like the workflow you have" (Dave2D — pulls viewer in)

## Tier Rating System (BTODtv — per-component clarity)

Use tier ratings for instant component-level judgment:

- "S tier seat, A tier back rest, A tier arms"
- "pretty high A tier all around"
- Creates clarity without long explanations

## Outro Phrases

- "There's no single perfect [X] for everyone. The right choice depends on [personal factors]."
- "So, there you have it." (Dave2D — ultra-simple, no fake excitement)
- "What are your picks? Are they aligned with ours? Are they completely different?" (BTODtv — invites disagreement)

### NEVER use:

- "And that's our list!"
- "If you enjoyed this video, don't forget to..."
- "Without further ado..."

## Sentence Rhythm Rules

From analysis of both channels:

1. **Spec sentences:** 15-35 words. Pack the data.
2. **Opinion sentences:** 2-6 words. Punch hard. "This is sick." "It's awesome."
3. **Trust sentences:** 10-20 words. Clear, specific limitation.
4. **Never:** 5+ sentences at the same length in a row.

## Natural Speech Markers (authenticity signals)

These make scripts sound human, not AI:

- "So," as sentence starter (common in both channels)
- Contractions: "it's", "you'll", "doesn't", "can't", "won't"
- "you got to" instead of "you have to"
- "kind of" / "sort of" for soft qualifiers
- "like" for comparison emphasis (sparingly)
- Short confirmations: "Right?" after a claim

## Alternative Structures

### Q&A Dialogue (Ahnestly — best for short-form / under 5 min):

- Two voices: viewer asks, expert answers
- Each product answers a SPECIFIC viewer need (not a ranking)
- "What if I'm on the heavier side?" → Aeron
- "I get really really hot" → mesh chair
- Products feel like personal advice, not a list

### Honorable Mentions Quickfire (Elliot Page — for top 10 lists):

- "Before we get into the top three, you know what we got to do? A quick fire round."
- 3 products in ~90 seconds before top 3 — builds anticipation

### Grouping Similar Products (Elliot Page):

- "I'm going to cheat a little bit. Okay, just allow it. It's the classics."
- Bundle 3-4 similar models into one slot to avoid repetition

## Anti-Patterns (from negative benchmark NwEexVMPH3I)

These destroy credibility:

1. Zero personal opinion = zero trust
2. Amazon listing language copy-pasted into script
3. "Unleash your creativity" / "Set the stage on fire" = press release
4. Every sentence 15-25 words = monotone
5. No transitions between products = slideshow, not narrative
6. "Are you looking for the best [X]?" opening = generic, AI-detectable

### 1. Comparison Format Drives Massive Views
1.4M views from a straightforward "X vs. Y" comparison. This is the most successful format studied. The curiosity-gap title ("You Might Not Like This") adds clickability. **Rayviews should consider occasional comparison videos alongside Top 5 rankings.**

### 2. Polished Studio = Major Trust Signal
The gray walls, framed art, plants, standing desk, and temperature display create an environment that signals professionalism and credibility. ### 3. Close-Up Mechanical Detail Shots Build Trust
The video dedicates significant time to close-up shots of chair mechanisms -- the Embody's spine, armrest joints, tilt systems. These shots say "I actually understand how this product works." **Dzine Img2Img should generate close-up detail variants for every product, focusing on the key engineering/quality feature.**

### 4. Short Duration, High Density = Algorithm-Friendly
At 6 minutes, this video is half the length of typical comparison content. Every second is information-dense with no filler. **Rayviews scripts at 1300-1800 words (8-11 min at 155 WPM) are already concise, but consider even tighter edits for comparison format.**

### 5. Showing Product Pages Signals Expertise
The btod.com product page screenshots serve double duty: they show detailed specs AND they signal that the presenter is a legitimate seller. **Rayviews could briefly flash Amazon product pages or manufacturer spec pages as trust-building moments -- but keep it brief (3-5 seconds max).**

### 6. Multiple Camera Angles from Simple Setup
The video appears to use 2-3 camera positions but achieves the feel of professional multi-cam production. Wide, medium, and close-up shots create visual variety. **For Ray avatar, this means generating at least 3 framings: wide (full workspace), medium (chest-up), and close-up (face only). Alternate between them during Lip Sync segments.**

### 7. Product as Visual Anchor
Each chair has a distinctive visual identity -- the Secretlab's black leather and logo, the Embody's blue woven spine. The video uses these visual signatures repeatedly. **Dzine product images should emphasize the single most distinctive visual feature of each product.**

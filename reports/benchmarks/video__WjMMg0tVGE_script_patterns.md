## Hook Style

Meta-complaint as hook — addresses viewer objection directly:

- "hey honestly I love your videos but I don't have time to watch a 2-hour long chair review just tell me what is the best chair"
- Immediately answers: "I think the best chair is the Herman Miller gaming Embody"

No buildup, no jargon lists. The hook IS the viewer's frustration. Brilliant for short-form.

## Structure: Q&A Dialogue Format

Two voices alternate — "viewer" asks, expert answers. Each question creates a new product recommendation:

1. "What is the best chair?" → Herman Miller Embody
2. "$2,000?! I struggle with lower back pain" → Anthos
3. "Do you have anything cheaper?" → Kaomami Atlas
4. "Does it come with that warranty stuff?" → Haworth Soji → Steelcase Amia
5. "I get really really hot" → Staples Dexley
6. "What if I'm on the heavier side?" → Herman Miller Aeron
7. "I'm an ergonomic rebel, I like to tuck my leg" → Steelcase Karman

Each product is triggered by a SPECIFIC viewer need, not arbitrary ranking. Zero filler.

## Key Techniques

### Need-driven product selection (not numbered):

Products aren't ranked #1-#5. Each is the answer to a specific problem:

- Back pain → Anthos
- Budget → Kaomami Atlas
- Hot → Staples Dexley
- Heavy → Aeron
- Fidgety → Steelcase Karman

**Takeaway:** This structure is more useful to viewers than arbitrary rankings. Consider for future scripts.

### Spec delivery — embedded in conversation, not listed:

- "it's expensive coming in at just under 2,000 bucks but it comes with a 12-year warranty"
- "This is a $260 chair but it has features and adjustments that you only see on chairs that are much much higher priced"
- "this thing has a fat seat cushion which means you know it's not going to compress quickly over time"

Specs feel like advice, not a spec sheet.

### Limitation delivery — natural and specific:

- "just keep in mind that it's got hard edges and the frame is stiff"
- "if you want like moving around throughout the day or you're a Tucker... it's not going to be great for you"
- Aeron limitation is extremely specific (tucking legs) — shows real usage experience.

### Trust anchors:

- "these guys have experience building wheelchairs so they know what it's like to have to sit all day long"
- "from what I could read online here in the United States staples's customer service is pretty good"
- "I mean this thing is the most famous chair in the world for a reason"
- Warranty + customer service + free returns mentioned for almost every chair — consistency builds trust.

## Opinion Markers

- "this thing is fantastic it feels like no other chair on the market"
- "I think this is the best headrest ever built"
- "ridiculously soft yet supportive"
- "my favorite Steelcase chair"
- "my favorite mesh chair under $200"
- "built like a tank"
- "the tried and true"

Short and unhedged. No "I would say" or "it's worth noting."

## Natural Speech Markers

- "okay um" (realistic hesitation)
- "you know" (conversational filler)
- "like" for emphasis ("if you want like moving around")
- "this thing" instead of "this product/chair"
- Contractions throughout
- "dude" (casual address)
- "what the" (genuine reaction)
- "a Tucker, single leg Tucker, double leg Tucker" (inventive vocabulary)

## Sentence Rhythm

Ultra-compressed. Almost no sentence over 25 words:

- "this thing is fantastic" (4 words)
- "but again pricey" (3 words)
- "I get it" (3 words)
- "excellent customer service 12-year warranty free returns" (rapid-fire spec list, no verbs)

## Patterns to ADOPT

1. **Q&A structure for short videos**: Each product answers a specific viewer question — eliminates need for ranking
2. **Need-driven recommendations**: "If you [specific problem], the [product] is going to be your pick"
3. **Warranty/service as trust pattern**: Mentioning warranty + returns for each product builds systematic trust
4. **Specific body-type recommendations**: "if you're over 150 lbs", "three different sizes a, b, c"
5. **"This thing" language**: Casual product reference ("this thing is fantastic") sounds more genuine than "this chair offers"
6. **Meta-hook**: Using the viewer's objection as the opening line

## Patterns to AVOID

1. None significant — this is an extremely tight script with zero waste
2. Only caveat: this format works for short videos (< 5 min). For 8-12 min scripts, would need expansion.
